Code for BTV Projects

Several projects are currently being developed and maintained by those involved in Code for BTV. If you're interested in becoming involved, you can join an existing team, or create a new one. You might want to consider a shovel-ready project, as it helps support civic organizations that have reached out to Code for BTV.

Current Projects

The following projects are under active development.

Lakecraft

ECHO Lake Aquarium & Science Center

Contributors: Nina Ridhibhinyo, John Cohn, Nicholas Floersch, Will Fallows

An educational tool that engages youth and adults in science by gamifying the Lake Champlain Basin. Using a custom modification to Minecraft, the group's goal is to eventually have a tool that may be used at the ECHO Center, at home, by educational institutions, and serve as a model to educators.

Deerfield Valley Rescue

Vermont Digital Economy Project

Contributors: Rob Fish, Timothy Clifford, Tabaré A. Gowon, Denise Puisto, Caitlyn Lovegrove, Dave Lovegrove

DVR's website was outdated and corrupted. The group gave DVR a total site makeover, using WordPress, and integrated Facebook and Paypal donation functionality.

Bethel Food Shelf

Vermont Digital Economy Project

Contributors: Rob Fish, Timothy Clifford, Tabaré A. Gowon, Denise Puisto, Caitlyn Lovegrove, Dave Lovegrove

The BFS had no web presence and no way to solicit for donations online. They also had no way to communicate their wish list. The team created a website that allows for donations to be made and makes it easy for BFS to update its wish list.

Green Up Vermont

Contributors: Justin Adams, Scott MacEwan, Danielle Steimke, Collin Dewitt, Evan Yandell, Ethan Eldridge, Joshua Dickerson, Paul Kiripolsky

During Green Up Vermont, many inefficiencies were identified: people covering the same ground multiples times, people not knowing where high need was, and not knowing where to drop off their garbage. The group created a web-based mobile user interface with server side service to collect data points as well as forums for discussion. A client-side data system allows for the organization of data points.

Burlington City Wiki

City of Burlington

Contributors: Micah Mutrux, Carl Irving, Nicholas Szumowski

The team created a central location where people may crowdsource information about Burlington. The wiki uses the MediaWiki platform and includes categories and a sampling of content. Importing and supporting extensions are in place (e.g., anti spam, social networking). The next step is to strategize a plan for content development and promotion of the wiki.

More

  • burlingtonwiki.com

School Juice

Burlington Electric Department

Contributors: John Cohn, Alan Peabody, Jason Baker, Dan Companion, Ted Holtz, Joe Golden, Justin Adams, John Stanton

The team brainstormed ways to begin providing electricity usage data from Burlington's new smart meters in some useful and open ways to the public. This information could be used by the public, by students and by the city to assess progress along the city's energy reduction goals. Our idea would be to present the energy usage data of all area schools in a way that can be searched, accessed by API and visualized.

Shovel-Ready Projects

The following are possible projects that have been requested by or otherwise noted as valuable to civic organizations. If you're interested in any of these projects, or if you have a project you'd like to share with us, please let us know: info@codeforbtv.org or @CodeForBTV

Adopt a Drain

Burlington Department of Public Works

The Burlington Department of Public Works has expressed interest in the implementation of an application that can facilitate their management and maintenance of storm water drains with the help of Burlington residents. Such an application already exists and was built in Ruby on Rails by OpenOakland, another Code for America Brigade.

Nonprofit Websites

Vermont Digital Economy Project

Vermont Digital Economy Project recognizes that communities that have nonprofits with a web presence are more resilient after a disaster, such as Tropical Storm Irene. Several websites for nonprofits have already been created via Code for BTV volunteers, and there are additional websites that are needed. The websites thus far have been built using WordPress.
